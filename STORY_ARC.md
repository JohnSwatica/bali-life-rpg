# STORY_ARC.md — Bali Life RPG (Draft v1)

> The progression spine: from arriving broke (新手村) to owning your villa, business, and bike — then the
> gates open to a shared multiplayer world where community becomes everything. This is the "drive" the game
> has been missing: a concrete dream to chase. Draft — see "Your calls" at the end.

## Premise

You land in Bali with a backpack, a few hundred thousand rupiah, and the dream half the internet has: to make
a life in paradise. No trust fund, no remote salary waiting. You'll hustle for every rupiah on a clapped-out
scooter, make friends who become family, and slowly build something of your own — until you own your corner of
Berawa. Then you open the gates to everyone else chasing the same thing.

The shape: **hustle (the verb) → people (the heart) → build (the ambition) → community (the world).**

---

## Act 0 — 新手村: Arrival  *(Tutorial)*

Dusk. You're dropped at a cheap *kos* (boarding room) with almost nothing. Your phone buzzes — **Ibu Sari**,
the warung auntie a mutual friend connected you with, who promised to help you land on your feet.

The **新手村任务** — each teaches one core verb, with the objective arrow on-screen the whole time:
1. **Walk to Ibu Sari's warung.** (Movement + following the waypoint.) She feeds you, explains how Berawa works.
2. **She lends you a beat-up scooter and signs you onto the gig app.** (Riding + the phone.)
3. **Your first delivery:** pick up pastries at **BAKED**, drop them at a villa down the lane. (The order loop:
   accept → ride to pickup → ride to dropoff → get paid + your first star rating.)
4. **Spend your first earnings:** grab a meal and a coffee. (Money + meters — energy, wellbeing.)
5. **Ride home and sleep.** (The day cycle; sleep restores energy.)

**Milestone:** *You survived your first day.* You have a scooter, the app, a few rupiah, and Ibu Sari in your
corner. 新手村 complete — the open hustle begins.

## Act 1 — The Hustle  *(Survival; the core loop)*

You're on your own now. Rent's due, you're broke, the scooter's barely holding together. Every day: wake,
hustle, eat, sleep — but you get faster, learn the streets, build your rating.

Through deliveries you keep crossing the same faces: **Kadek** (the baker at BAKED), **Ari** (a fellow nomad
at the coworking), **Made** (a local who knows everyone). First light relationship beats. You find a café you
keep coming back to. The world throws curveballs — rush hour, a fragile-cargo run, a flaky customer, a sudden
downpour that empties the streets and spikes the fares.

*Gameplay:* the delivery loop + star rating + your first scooter upgrade + the meters/budget squeeze. A good
rating unlocks better gigs.

**Milestone:** *You've found your feet.* Stable income, a solid rating, and you move out of the cramped kos
into a proper room. Not rich — but no longer drowning.

## Act 2 — Finding Your People  *(The heart)*

With breathing room, life becomes about more than rent. Ari pulls you into the coworking crew at **Tropical
Nomad**; Made drags you to the **Berawa run crew** and the sunset circle at the beach; you become a regular at
**Milk & Madu** and **FINNS**.

You go from "that delivery guy" to *one of us.* Relationship arcs pay off — a real crew, inside jokes, maybe
someone you're falling for. Your standing in the scene grows (the trust graph — the Nomad Nest seed). People
tip you off to opportunities, do you favors, invite you in.

*Gameplay:* social activities, events, clubs, relationship arcs; social standing unlocks premium gigs and
perks. The real tension: the hustle pays rent, but the people and the beach are *why you're here.*

**Milestone:** *You've got a crew and a name.* Over beers at sunset, someone says it: "Why are you still
running deliveries on someone else's app? Start your own thing. And a few of us have been talking about getting
a villa together…" The dream is lit.

## Act 3 — Building Something  *(The climb)*

You decide to stop hustling for others and build your own — two very-Bali dreams: your own **warung that grows
into a café**, and your own **villa** instead of a rented room. And a **real bike** to retire the rattletrap.

The grind gets purposeful. You scrape capital — your savings, maybe your crew chips in (the social payoff
funding the ambition), a lease on a tiny kitchen. Then the obstacles, all authentic: permits and
"administration," a competitor, a supplier who flakes, the wet season threatening your opening. And your crew
shows up — Kadek in the kitchen, Made working the landlord, Ari building your buzz.

*Gameplay:* a business-building layer (stock it, run it, grow it — the café tycoon), big money goals, saving
toward the villa, the bike upgrade. The hustle continues, but now it funds a dream, not just rent.

**Milestone:** *Grand opening.* Your place opens its doors, and your neighborhood shows up.

## Act 4 — The Good Life  *(The dream, realized — the solo "win")*

You own your **villa.** Your **business** is running and known. You ride a **bike** you're proud of. You
arrived with a backpack; now you own a corner of Berawa. This is the solo game's victory.

And then, quiet. Sitting on your terrace, you realize the money was never the point — it was the mornings at
the warung, the crew at sunset, the people. The game asks: *now that you've built your life, what do you do
with it?*

**The hinge:** *You've built your place in Berawa. Time to open the gates.* → **Multiplayer unlocks.**

## Act 5 — The Open World  *(Multiplayer; social-first; Nomad Nest)*

The world fills with **real people.** Your villa becomes a place you can **host.** Your café serves real
players. Your crew can include real friends. The Berawa you built is now a living, shared neighborhood.

The focus flips: money's no longer the point — **community is.** You run your club, host events, throw villa
parties, sponsor newcomers' grand openings. Real shop-owners promote their events and deals. And newcomers
arrive in their *own* 新手村 — where **you can be the Ibu Sari** for someone just landing.

This is where **Nomad Nest** fully blooms: you meet other real nomads as avatars, vibe-match on lifestyle and
trust, and **"hatch" into households** — the villa-shares and crews the real app turns into actual rentals. The
game becomes the front door to the marketplace: *you live alongside people here before you live with them
there.* The endgame is community — your reputation, your relationships, your place among real humans.

## How this maps to the build

- **Act 0 (新手村):** the tutorial + the scooter-hustle core + guided onboarding *(current salvage sprint)*.
- **Act 1 (Hustle):** the delivery loop, opportunity engine, rating, upgrades *(current / near-term)*.
- **Act 2 (People):** the social layer — events, clubs, relationship arcs — **repurposed as the heart** *(built; needs re-wiring to this arc)*.
- **Act 3 (Build):** the business/café layer + villa saving + bike upgrade *(new — the ambition layer)*.
- **Act 4 (Good Life):** the win-state milestone + the multiplayer hinge.
- **Act 5 (Open World):** multiplayer + Nomad Nest *(VISION roadmap C–F — the far future; doors kept open, not built yet)*.

Each act ends on a concrete milestone so the player always has a goal and a reason for the next session — the
exact thing missing today.
