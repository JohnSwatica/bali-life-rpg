> This document owns the GAME: the verbs, the map, what every interactable

## 1. DESIGN THESIS — FIVE VERBS

Every interactable must serve at least one of five player verbs. Anything
serving zero verbs is cut or rebuilt. The current build fails this test:
its only verb is "read a menu."

- RIDE — the scooter is a skill surface: traffic, corners, cargo care,
  rain, fuel, alleys. Every delivery is PLAYED, never teleported. (GTA:CW)
- TALK — conversations with real choices, gifts, favors; the two-axis
  reputation makes HOW you talk matter. (Stardew)
- SERVE — craft under pressure: pastry runs, café service, your own café's
  daily rush. (Stardew / Overcooked-lite)
- UNCOVER — the Elena/Rumah mystery hunted physically: hidden notebook
  pages, overheard scenes, an investigation board. (Pokémon + adventure)
- BUILD — spaces you own that visibly change: kos → room → warung → café →
  villa; furniture placement that affects play. (Stardew)

THE REBUILD RULE: every venue interaction becomes a SCENE (enter an
interior; people are there; something is happening), a PLAYABLE BEAT
(mini-game), or both. Meter effects are byproducts of play, never the
content itself. "Coffee at Milk & Madu" = walk in, see who is at the
tables (schedule-driven), sit with someone (TALK) or take the corner and
work (Focus Sprint). The +Focus happens BECAUSE you did something.

## 2. THE STORY ARC AS LIVED EXPERIENCE

STORY_BIBLE v3's spine, written as what the player DOES. Each act has a
goal, an antagonist pressure, a map unlock, a system unlock, mini-games
introduced, and a non-combat FINALE SETPIECE (our "gym badge").

### ACT 0 — "The Borrowed Scooter" (30-45 min, Day 1)
Opens in MORNING light (night falls at the END of the tutorial day as
payoff). Scripted quiet walk-in to Canggu Station, no UI until the phone
buzzes. Find Ibu Sari (arrow-led, mandatory). She feeds you (warung
INTERIOR scene, first door you walk through), lends the scooter; notebook
+ SIM under the seat. First delivery = the RIDE tutorial (one traffic
decision, one corner, cargo meter, ~90s, fail-forward). Evening: learn
the phone, sleep in a genuinely bleak kos room (the BUILD motivator).
Planted: Kadek's aborted "hey, that's-", the Berawa 2.0 billboard, rent
countdown. Finale: sleep -> tomorrow's gigs -> ACT 1 card.

### ACT 1 — "The Hustle" (~90 min, Days 2-5)
Goal: make rent (Rp 450, Day 4), hit rating 4.0, escape the kos. Map: The
Corner + the Strip only; beach socially gated. Systems: the morning hand
(3-5 dawn gigs), scooter condition + repair mini-game, energy budgeting.
Rio taunts by text; mid-act SETPIECE: street race vs Rio (pure RIDE).
Mystery: notebook page 2; the Rumah ruin is discoverable, fresh canang
sari at its base. Moral fork: the No-Questions Package, staged as a scene
at the rental. Scripted: a rate-cut event; a Day-3 storm debuts the
Rainy-Night Run. Finale: rent day scene -> Sari's warung dinner, whole
cast, "you remind me of someone" -> new room (first furniture) -> ACT 2.

### ACT 2 — "Finding Your People" (~2-2.5 hrs, Days 6-12)
Goal: join two crews, become a "regular," earn a seat at the Sunday
sunset circle. Map unlock: Beach + FINNS; paddies visible from the road,
one patch of rice yellowing. Systems: crews on a WEEKLY CALENDAR (Mon
dawn run, Tue pizza night, Wed pastry crunch, Thu cowork sprint, Fri
surge, Sun sunset circle), gifts, relationship tiers with authored
scene-beats. Mini-games: Surf Session, Focus Sprint, Market Haggle.
Mystery: Elena's story assembles through relationship beats; Threads
matures into an INVESTIGATION BOARD. Antagonist: Pak Bagus intro at FINNS;
midpoint his OFFER (central fork opens); Ari's brand-deal shame; Rio
promoted to Jalan Captain. Finale: sunset bonfire, "Rumah" said aloud,
Sari gives you Elena's outline -> ACT 3.

### ACT 3 — "Building Something" (~2.5 hrs, Days 13-24)
Goal: open the warung->café, keep it alive, find Elena. Map unlock:
paddies/subak district; the Berawa 2.0 hoarding visibly GROWS, eating a
stall you know. Systems (business layer unlocks here): the legal fork
(fast nominee vs slow banjar-blessed partnership via Made); fit-out at
Bungalow Living where FURNITURE PLACEMENT affects service flow; menu from
Kadek's recipes; daily open/close with Café Service; Kadek joins as staff.
Rival: Rio's café opens next door, daily numbers on a chalkboard;
poach-Kadek fork. Mystery near-payoff: phone-shop kid unlocks the SIM ->
Elena answers from Lombok -> the truth (the subak method, aimed at The
Corner now). Finale: engineered permit shutdown on launch week
(Permit-Office Gauntlet); the community signs on, the Co-op is born;
reopening night is a full Café Service Rush -> ACT 4.

### ACT 4 — "The Good Life" (~2 hrs, Days 25-32)
Goal: villa + proper bike affordable, same week Bagus forces the endgame.
Convergence: walk the channel, find the diversion gate (Subak Channel
Repair — the non-combat final dungeon); the banjar hearing (a dialogue
gauntlet where every flag/tier/axis you earned is your character sheet);
Elena returns for the reopening (Offering-Making, reconciliation with
Sari); Bagus's face-saving turn depends on how you played him. Finale:
villa keys on the café table, cast gathered, paddies green again. "The
money was never the point."

### ACT 5 — The Open Door (endless)
A new backpacker steps off; Sari looks at you; you lend the scooter. Café
runs weekly, ceremonies cycle annually (Galungan; Nyepi as a full-stop
silent day), the Co-op persists. Retention endgame + future-multiplayer
fiction.

## 3. THE MAP

One continuous world, SIX districts, unlocked act-by-act via soft social
gates (NPC lines, not walls). Target ~40 venues, of which 14 are
enterable interiors.

Districts: THE CORNER (Canggu Station, Warung Sari, kos, Rumah ruin) ·
THE STRIP (the S-curve spine road: BAKED, Milk & Madu, Satu-Satu, scooter
rental, Bungalow Living, phone shop, market) · THE BEACH (Berawa Beach,
FINNS, beach clubs, sunset point, surf racks, bonfire circle) · VILLA
LANES (walled villas, delivery gates, your villa) · PADDIES & SUBAK (rice
terraces, channel, farmer huts, the gate) · BERAWA 2.0 (construction zone,
sales office — physically ADVANCES between acts).

Map rules:
- The plaza-grid dies. The center becomes a real two-lane street with
  sidewalks, warung fronts flush to the walk, parked scooters, and ALLEYS
  as shortcuts (RIDE mastery = knowing the alleys).
- Landmark navigation: the banyan at The Corner, the FINNS tower, the
  temple gate — visible above rooftops so the player navigates by WORLD,
  arrow as backup only.
- The antagonist is SPATIAL: the 2.0 hoarding advances between acts; the
  player watches the map lose territory, no dialogue needed.
- Doors read as doors: awning + door tile + light spill on every
  enterable venue (Pokémon grammar).
- Weather/clock matter: rain slicks corners; night changes who's out;
  Nyepi empties the street for one scripted day.

## 5. PRESENTATION

- Interiors: Pokémon room grammar. Door -> 400ms fade -> a small dense
  room (~12x8 tiles) -> exit mat. One thing happening.
- Dialogue: portrait bust (flat-color, 8 needed) + name + text + 2-4
  choice buttons max. No text walls. Ambient bubbles only for flavor.
- Cutscene grammar: letterbox bars + scripted walk + camera pan (existing
  tweens). Used for act cards, the 6 finale setpieces, first meetings.
- The morning hand: on wake the phone auto-opens with today's 3-5 cards.
  One decision screen (Stardew's "what's today about"). Close it, day
  starts.
- HUD: minimal, corner-docked, contextual (status chip, objective chip,
  small minimap, four micro meter-bars, contextual "E — verb" prompt,
  queued toasts). Under ~8% of the frame while roaming.
- Feedback is diegetic first: café queue length, Rio's chalkboard, the
  yellowing rice, the advancing hoarding — the world reports state before
  any toast.
- Art: commit to the warm flat-color language — locked palette, 32px
  tiles, character shadows, 3-frame walk cycles, door/awning grammar.
  Portraits can start as flat-color busts.

## 6. MINI-GAME ROSTER — 12, IN THREE TIERS

TIER 1 CORE (played constantly; must be deep):
1. Delivery Riding — the RIDE verb: traffic, corners, cargo, fuel, alleys,
   weather. Every gig. The single biggest build in the game.
2. Café Service Rush — the SERVE endgame: seat/serve/clear under a timer,
   layout-dependent. Daily from Act 3.
3. Conversation choices — TALK as play: choice + timing + axis-gated
   options ("[Rooted] Mention the subak" only if earned).

TIER 2 SITUATIONAL (weekly-ish, act-introduced):
4. Rainy-Night Run (A1) — Delivery Riding, rain + dark + fragile cargo.
5. Wrench Repair (A1) — timing bar at the rental.
6. Surf Session (A2) — balance-timing, capped weekly.
7. Focus Sprint (A2) — rhythm-timing at Satu-Satu.
8. Market Haggle (A2) — push/charm/walk-away against a price tell.

TIER 3 SETPIECE (once or twice, story-staged):
9. Race vs Rio (A1, again A3) — head-to-head Delivery Riding.
10. Permit-Office Gauntlet (A3) — dialogue gauntlet vs bureaucracy.
11. Subak Channel Repair (A4) — communal finale; timed valve sequence.
12. Offering-Making (A4+) — assemble canang sari; the ceremony frame.

Cadence: Tier 1 is the loop, Tier 2 is the week's texture, Tier 3 is
earned punctuation. Nothing in Tier 2/3 is ever demanded twice in a row.

## 7. BUILD ORDER (layers)

Existing substrate survives (meters, saves, opportunities, reputation
axes, relationships, phone data). The WORLD and its VERBS get rebuilt.
- L1 World Rebuild: interior/door primitive, the 14 interior shells, new
  street/district geometry, morning start, tone fixes. (Delivered in
  steps — see the L1 packets.)
- L2 Presentation Kit: dialogue trees + portraits, cutscene primitives,
  full-screen phone PDA, HUD diet.
- L3 Day Engine: morning hand, weekly calendar, full NPC schedules.
- L4 RIDE: delivery riding as real gameplay + repair + the race variant.
- L5 TALK: relationship tracks as scenes with choices; gifts; Act 0-2
  story mounted on top.
- L6 Act 2 content complete: crews, calendar events, Surf/Focus/Haggle,
  Bagus's offer arc.
- L7 BUILD + SERVE: business layer + Café Service — Act 3 playable.
- L8 Act 4 setpieces + endgame + Act 5 loop.

Each layer ends with a designer screenshot pass against the three
benchmarks (GTA: Chinatown Wars, Stardew Valley, Pokémon).
